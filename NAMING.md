# Naming & positioning — working doc

The product: a curated library of design systems written as plain `DESIGN.md` files that AI coding agents read to build every screen in a consistent look. Browse, copy, build.

The audience: **AI builders** — the people shipping real products with agents (Claude Code, Cursor, v0). Designers who build, engineers with taste, solo founders. The framing JK wants: **forward-deployed creatives**. A riff on the forward-deployed engineer. The creative who sits at the front, ships, and carries taste into the build.

Voice reference: **Reaktor**. Confident, direct, short punchy statements, active language, no hedging, outcome over process, dashes for momentum.

---

## The core idea, in one line

> Your agent can build anything. It can't taste. This gives it taste, as a file.

That tension — infinite capability, zero judgement — is the whole pitch. Everything else hangs off it.

---

## Names

### Recommendation: keep **design.md** as the wordmark
It *is* the thing. A filename is the most honest, dev-native name possible — it reads as a real artefact, not a brand. Like `robots.txt` or `.env`, the format becomes the brand. Lowercase, monospace-friendly, instantly legible to the exact audience. Hard to beat for this crowd.

### If we want a true product name (shortlist)
| Name | Why it works | Watch-out |
|---|---|---|
| **Specimen** | Type-specimen heritage; the site literally renders live specimens. Elegant, design-credible, ownable. | Slightly fine-art, less dev-native. |
| **House Style** | A brand's house style — "give your AI a house style." Human, editorial, memorable. | Two words; .com likely gone. |
| **Blueprint** | Build from a blueprint. Maker energy, AI-builder fit. | Common word, crowded. |
| **Stencil** | The agent fills the stencil. Strong metaphor for spec-driven generation. | A JS framework already owns it. |
| **Atlas** | A curated atlas of design systems. Confident, collectable. | Overused in tech. |

**Verdict:** ship as `design.md`. If a brandable name is wanted later, **Specimen** is the one to test.

## The "forward-deployed creative" — a positioning note

Worth owning this phrase. The forward-deployed engineer embeds with the customer and ships. The forward-deployed creative does the same with taste: sits at the front of the build, makes the calls, ships the look. This tool is their kit. It's also, not coincidentally, exactly the role JK is positioning himself for. The product and the personal brand point the same way.
